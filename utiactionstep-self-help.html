Self Help Strategies

While you're waiting for your urine culture results and/or for antibiotics to kick in, there are several things that you can do to help reduce your bladder symptoms>

Hydration

It's VERY important to drink your normal 6 to 8 glasses of water to dilute your urine and flush your bladder of bacteria.

Avoid Cranberry Products

For the patient with the uber sensitive bladder and/or IC, cranberry is harsh and often quite painful. It's an old wives tale that cranberry can help prevent infections. Research has shown little effectiveness. On the otherhand, D-Mannose has modest evidence showing that it might help reduce e-coli based infections.

AZO Bladder Pain Relief Tablets

AZO Bladder Pain Relief Tablets may help reduce the symptoms and discomfort of an infection. It is not, however, an antibiotic. It is ONLY used to reduce the discomfort associated with a UTI.

Heat therapy

Use a heating pad and/or, take a warm shower to help relax your pelvic floor muscles. Muscles naturally tighten as a response to pain which can create more discomfort. Heat may help relax those muscles.

Pain Medication

Infections can be very painful thus the use of a short-term pain medication is often suggested and would be prescribed by your physician. AZO Bladder Pain Relief Tablets may also help reduce pain and discomfort.

CAUTION - Please contact your physician if you have any alarming symptoms and/or if they don't improve within a reasonable amount of time. Bacterial infections can be notoriously difficult to treat due to the rise in antibiotic resistent infections.

Bladder Wall Irritation/Inflammation Flares

Action Steps

#1 - Hydration

Drink a normal 6 to 8 glasses of water to dilute your urine and reduce acid levels, if present.

#2 - Diet Modification

Most newly diagnosed patients are given a list of foods to avoid, particularly foods high in acids & caffeines (soda, coffee's, teas, green teas, citrus, tomato, etc.) If you're unclear about what foods are bladder friendly vs. bladder irritating, see the ICN IC & Prostatitis Diet List

#3 - Alkalyze Urine

If you've been eating high acidic foods, you can try to reduce the acid by using Prelief or 1/4 tsp of baking soda in a regular glass of water. This can reduce the impact of the acid on your bladder wall but it should not be done more than once a day. Baking soda is high in sodium which can contribute to high blood pressure.

#4 - OTC Bladder Pain Relievers

AZO Bladder Pain Relief tablets act by numbing the bladder wall and may provide some relief. This is the OTC version of the medication Pyridium and will turn urine a dark orange color.

#5 - Heat therapy

Use a heating pad, take a warm shower to help relax your pelvic floor muscles. Muscles naturally tighten as a response to pain which can create more discomfort. Heat may help relax those muscles.

#6 - Antispasmodics

If you struggle with bladder spasms (a sharp cramping that occurs at the end of urination), doctors may provide an antispasmodic medication like Ditropan. These can calm spasms in as a little as 30 minutes but, if used over a long period of time, may make it more difficult to urinate.

#7 - Pain Medication

As prescribed by your physician. Bladder wall pain can be excruciating for some patients and has been rated equivalent to cancer pain. The American Urological Association encourages physicians to not only assess pain at every clinical visit but also to treat pain compassionately and promptly using a variety of medications, including opiate medication if necessary.
